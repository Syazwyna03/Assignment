Step-by-Step Guide

1. After completing your Bachelor of New Media Communication, you need to decide whether to pursue a law degree because your degree is not in law.
2. Apply for admission to the Bachelor of Laws (LLB) programme at the University of Malaya (UM). Applications are highly competitive and require excellent academic achievement.
3. If the application is successful, need to attend an interview and written test conducted by the Faculty of Law, UM as part of the screening process.
4. Once accepted, you will follow the 4-year LLB programme at UM which covers core subjects such as Constitutional Law, Criminal Law, Contract Law and many more.
5. Complete chambering/pupillage under a law firm.
6. Get called to the Bar and start practicing as a lawyer or open my on firm.
